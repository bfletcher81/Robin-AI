# Robin AI

**Robbing AI from the overlords. Pulling on Robin Hood. And it's called Robin — like a little bird on your shoulder.**

Robin is an open source, privacy-first personal AI companion designed to run locally on your own hardware. No subscriptions. No cloud dependency. No surveillance. Just a small, capable intelligence that knows you, serves only you, and belongs entirely to you.

**Intelligence should be owned, not rented.**

---

## The Problem

The AI industry is built on a simple premise: you rent access to intelligence. Monthly. Forever. The intelligence gets better, but so does the lock-in. The price never goes down. You never own anything.

Your most intimate thoughts, decisions, and context flow through servers owned by corporations whose interests are not aligned with yours. The tool watches the craftsman and reports back to the toolmaker.

Tools should serve the person using them — not the other way around.

## The Vision

Robin is a personal AI thinking partner that:

- **Runs locally** on hardware you own — no cloud, no call home, no dependency
- **Knows you deeply** — your context, your history, your goals, your life — and uses that knowledge to serve only you
- **Is always present** — ambient, conversational, fluid — not summoned like a servant but present like a companion
- **Is fully open source** — GPL v3, auditable, forkable, improvable, and permanently free
- **Belongs to you** — your data never leaves your device, your model answers to no one but you

Think of it as the right to repair, but for AI. The right to *own* your own thinking partner. Not lease it.

## Why "Robin"?

The name works on every level:

- **Robin Hood** — taking intelligence back from the powerful and giving it to everyone
- **The bird** — small, ubiquitous, shows up every morning without being asked, sits on your shoulder
- **The sidekick** — not the hero, doesn't try to be, just always there backing you up
- **Round robin** — something that circulates, everyone gets a turn — which is exactly what open source is

## Philosophy

The model is increasingly commodity. What matters is the **personal context layer** — the thing that makes a small local model more useful to *you* than a massive remote model that knows nothing about you.

A fine-tuned 7B model loaded with your life context will outperform a 400B model that treats you like a stranger.

Small, specialised, personal beats large, general, remote. Every time.

## Principles

1. **Open source, always.** GPL v3. The freedom is viral. Anyone who touches Robin must keep Robin free.
2. **Privacy by architecture.** Not by policy, not by promise — by design. Your data never leaves your hardware.
3. **Owned, not rented.** No subscriptions. No accounts. No terms of service that change underneath you.
4. **Radical accessibility.** A well-documented, reproducible build that a reasonably technical person can assemble for a few hundred quid.
5. **Community-built.** Robin grows the way Linux grew — not because one company pushed it, but because it was useful, it was free, and it was yours.
